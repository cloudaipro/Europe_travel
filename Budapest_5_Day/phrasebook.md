# Hungarian Phrasebook — Budapest Survival

Hungarian (`magyar`) is a Uralic language unrelated to its neighbours — it sounds nothing like Slavic or Germanic. English is widely spoken in central Budapest among under-40s and in tourism, but a few words go a long way.

Back to [itinerary](Budapest_5_Day_Itinerary.md).

## Pronunciation cheat

- `a` = "o" as in "hot" (short)
- `á` = "ah" as in "father" (long)
- `e` = "eh" as in "bed"
- `é` = "ay" as in "hey"
- `i` / `í` = "ee"
- `o` / `ó` = "oh"
- `ö` / `ő` = like German "ö" — round lips, say "eh"
- `u` / `ú` = "oo"
- `ü` / `ű` = like German "ü" — round lips, say "ee"
- `s` = "sh" (always)
- `sz` = "s" (always)
- `c` = "ts"
- `cs` = "ch"
- `gy` = soft "dy" (like the "d" in "during" British English)
- `ny` = "ny" as in "canyon"
- `ty` = soft "ty"
- `zs` = "zh" as in "treasure"
- **Stress** is **always on the first syllable** — this is the single most important rule.

## Polite phrases that punch above their weight

- **Tessék** (TESH-shayk) — universal "here you go / what would you like / pardon?". Hungarians use it constantly.
- **Bocsi** (BOH-chee) — casual "sorry"; younger people use this with friends.
- **Csókolom** (CHO-koh-lom) — formal greeting from younger to older woman; means "I kiss [your hand]". Old-school but still warm.
- **Jó étvágyat!** (yoh AYT-vah-djot) — "Good appetite!" — say this to your waiter or table neighbour.
- **Hajrá Magyarország!** (HOY-rah MOD-yor-or-sahg) — "Go Hungary!" — handy at football bars.
