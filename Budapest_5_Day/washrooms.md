# Budapest Washroom Reference

Budapest's public toilet game is decent but mostly **paid**. Rule of thumb plus a stop-by-stop free-WC index for the 5-day route.

Back to [itinerary](Budapest_5_Day_Itinerary.md).

## Rules of thumb

- **Always carry 200–500 HUF coins.** Most attendants reject paper bills and won't make change.
- **Café customer = free WC.** A 1,200 HUF espresso buys you a clean toilet anywhere central.
- **Museums = free with ticket.** Use them on the way out, not the way in.
- **Malls = always free.** Mammut (Buda), Westend (near Nyugati), Aréna Mall (City Park east), Etele Plaza (south Buda).
- **Metro stations:** M1 stations have free attendant-staffed WCs. M2/M3/M4 are mostly paid (200 HUF).
- **Bath complexes:** free with admission ticket.
- **Restaurants:** free for diners; some refuse non-customers, but a polite *"Bocsánat, lehet WC?"* often gets a yes.

## App + map

- **mywcindex.hu** — most public toilets with hours and prices.
- Google Maps "public toilet near me" works but underreports the unofficial-but-friendly café option.

## Free WC by day

### Arrival — Kelenföld → Astoria

- **Kelenföld pályaudvar** — paid WC 250 HUF on the M4 mezzanine.
- **Kálvin tér M3/M4 station** — paid 200 HUF.
- **Astoria area** — your hotel = free obviously. Otherwise paid 200 HUF at Astoria metro.

### Day 1 (Fri) — Pest icons + Danube cruise

- **Lunch spots near Astoria** (Bors GasztroBár, Spinoza, Stand by Me) — free with order.
- **Hotel Marriott** lobby (Apáczai Csere János u. 4) — free, no questions.
- **St Stephen's Basilica** — paid 300 HUF inside, or free at Erzsébet tér ground-floor terminal (under Akvárium Klub).
- **Vörösmarty tér** — Café Gerbeaud free with order, or M1 station below paid 400 HUF.
- **Legenda cruise boat** — onboard, free.

### Day 2 (Sat) — Parliament + Andrássy + City Park + Bath

- **Hungarian Parliament** — Visitor Centre (with tour ticket), free + clean.
- **Hungarian State Opera lobby** — free during box-office hours.
- **Oktogon kiosk** — paid 300 HUF.
- **Heroes Square** — no WC at the monument. Free in **Műcsarnok** lobby (any ticket) and **Vajdahunyad Castle café terrace** (paid 300 HUF).
- **House of Music** — free with ticket, very clean.
- **Széchenyi Bath** — free with admission, multiple inside.
- **Robinson Restaurant** (dinner) — free with order.

### Day 3 (Sun) — Buda Castle + Gellért + ruin bars

- **Matthias Church** — free with ticket.
- **Hospital in the Rock** — free with ticket; paid 200 HUF outside lobby.
- **Castle Hill terrace cafés** — free with order. **Hilton Budapest** lobby (Hess András tér 1-3) has clean free WCs — non-guests usually allowed if you walk in confidently.
- **Citadella plateau kiosk** — paid 300 HUF, closes 20:00. After that walk down to **Hotel Gellért** lobby (free with espresso) or paid Liberty Bridge tram-stop kiosk.
- **Karaván** — paid 200 HUF (long Sun queue). All four ruin bars (Szimpla, Doblo, Mazel Tov, Mika Tivadar) have free WCs with a drink.

### Day 4 (Mon) — Central Market + Palace District + Margaret Island + Tram 2

- **Central Market Hall** — paid 300 HUF inside the entrance, or free with order at upstairs **Fakanál Étterem**.
- **Hungarian National Museum exterior** — closed Mon, no toilet access; use Café Csiga (Vásár u. 2, free with order).
- **Mikszáth Kálmán tér cafés** — free with order.
- **Astoria metro** — paid 200 HUF.
- **Párisi Udvar Hotel lobby** — free with coffee at Brasserie & Atelier (~2,500 HUF).
- **Vigadó tér / Marriott** — free.
- **Margaret Island south entrance bike kiosk** — paid 200 HUF.
- **Margaret Island Musical Fountain** — paid 200 HUF.
- **Hotel Hélia** (north tip of island) — free in lobby with a coffee.

### Day 5 (Tue) — Szentendre full day

- **Batthyány tér HÉV station** — paid 200 HUF (use before 40-min train ride).
- **Onboard HÉV** — no toilet on H5 trains; go before boarding.
- **Szentendre HÉV station** — paid 200 HUF.
- **Szentendre TourInform office** (Dumtsa Jenő u.) — free, the easiest free option in town.
- **Szentendre town hall** (Városháza, Fő tér) — free public WC during business hours (closes 16:00).
- **Café Edeni** (Fő tér 8) — free with order; the practical Fő tér option.
- **Templom-domb (Church Hill)** — no WC up there. Go before climbing.
- **Belgrád Cathedral courtyard** — free with ticket entry.
- **Lánchíd Söröző** (lunch) — free with order.
- **Kovács Margit Museum** — free with ticket.
- **Back in Budapest evening:** free at hotel; farewell dinner restaurant free with order.

### Departure — Wed pre-airport

- **Hotel** — last free WC before taxi.
- **BUD airport landside** — paid 300 HUF.
- **BUD airside** — free in every concourse, post-security.

## Practical etiquette

- Most paid WCs have an attendant. Drop the coin in the saucer, smile, say *köszönöm*.
- Toilet paper supply is reliable centrally; no need to carry a backup, but pack tissues for Szentendre.
- Hand-dryer / soap availability is unreliable in market halls — pocket pack of tissues + sanitiser worth carrying.
- *"Hol van a WC?"* (HOL von o VAY-tsay) gets you pointed in the right direction anywhere.
