Vienna Travel Itinerary

May 18 – May 22 | Local & Vlogger Secrets

🎒 Pre-Trip Checklist

  • Download WienMobil App: Buy your 72-hour transit pass here. No turnstiles, but strict undercover checkers!
  • Cash is King: Go to an ATM upon arrival. Many traditional spots on this list are cash-only.
  • Tipping Etiquette: Don't leave money on the table. Tell the waiter the total you want to pay (rounding up 5-10%).

🛬 Day 1: May 18 (Sunday) – Arrival & The First Authentic Bite

You will arrive at 5:00 PM. By the time you drop your bags, it will be 6:30 PM. Let's dive straight into local culture.

🥩 7:30 PM – Dinner at Gastwirtschaft Steman

📍 Otto-Bauer-Gasse 7, 1060 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot Recommended by local chefs. This 100-year-old traditional Wirtshaus (tavern) has zero tourist lines and an incredibly authentic, wood-paneled atmosphere.

The Food Skip the tourist traps and get your first real Viennese Schnitzel here. Perfectly wavy, golden-fried veal. Squeeze fresh lemon over it and eat it alongside a traditional vinegary potato salad.

🌆 9:00 PM – The Vibe at Museumsquartier (MQ)

📍 Museumsplatz 1, 1070 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot Locals hang out in the inner courtyard on the "Enzis" (giant, geometric lounge chairs). Grab a drink from the kiosk and sit under the evening lights to soak in the local Sunday night atmosphere.

☕ Day 2: May 19 (Monday) – Trams, Grandeur, and Imperial Feasts

🍰 9:30 AM – Breakfast at Café Korb

📍 Brandstätte 9, 1010 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot A mid-century modern coffeehouse beloved by local artists and writers. Experience the famous Wiener Schmäh (dry, charming waiter humor).

The Food Order the Apfelstrudel sitting in a pool of vanilla sauce, paired with an Einspänner (black coffee topped with cold whipped cream).

🚋 11:00 AM – The D-Tram Sightseeing Hack

📍 Start near Oper: Opernring 2, 1010 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Hack Instead of a €35 tourist bus, board public Tram Line D using your transit pass. It loops directly around the Ringstraße, giving front-row views of the Opera House, Parliament, and Hofburg Palace.

🏭 3:30 PM – Quirky Architecture at Spittelau

📍 Spittelauer Lände 45, 1090 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot Take the U-Bahn to Spittelau. Designed by eccentric architect Hundertwasser, this functioning waste plant looks like a psychedelic Willy Wonka factory. A massive hidden gem for photos.

🍲 7:00 PM – Dinner at Plachutta (Wollzeile)

📍 Wollzeile 38, 1010 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Food Emperor Franz Joseph’s favorite dish: Tafelspitz. Follow the 3 steps: 1) Drink the beef consommé. 2) Spread hot bone marrow onto toasted rye bread. 3) Eat the tender boiled beef with apple-horseradish.

🌿 Day 3: May 20 (Tuesday) – Hipster Grätzel & Vineyard Sunsets

👵 9:30 AM – Breakfast at Vollpension

📍 Schleifmühlgasse 16, 1040 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot A massive hit on YouTube. Designed like a retro living room and staffed entirely by local grandmas and grandpas (Omas and Opas) baking their own family cake recipes.

🌇 1:30 PM – Free Views at IKEA Westbahnhof

📍 Europaplatz 1, 1150 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Hack Skip expensive tower entry fees. Take the escalator to the top of the eco-friendly, car-free IKEA. It has a completely free, public rooftop terrace offering spectacular panoramic views of Vienna.

🍇 4:00 PM – Stadtwanderweg 1 & Heuriger Dinner

📍 Trail Start: Zahnradbahnstraße, 1190 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

📍 Dinner at Mayer am Pfarrplatz: Pfarrplatz 2, 1190 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Excursion Take the D-Tram to Nußdorf and walk up the Stadtwanderweg 1 trail through active vineyards. Stop at a Heuriger (wine tavern) like Mayer am Pfarrplatz. Build a charcuterie board and drink Gemischter Satz (local white wine).

🌊 Day 4: May 21 (Wednesday) – Rivers, Markets & The Emperor's Mess

🛍️ 10:00 AM – Karmelitermarkt

📍 Karmelitermarkt, 1020 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot Skip the touristy Naschmarkt. Karmelitermarkt is a booming, trendy local market. Grab a coffee and browse the stalls.

⛵ 3:00 PM – Boating on the Alte Donau (Old Danube)

📍 U-Bahn Alte Donau: Wagramer Str., 1220 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Spot Vienna’s water comes from the Alps. The Old Danube is a calm offshoot. Rent a retro electric boat to cruise the water and look at the skyline—a true local activity.

🥞 6:00 PM – The Sweet Finale at Demel

📍 Kohlmarkt 14, 1010 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Food "The Emperor's Mess" (Kaiserschmarrn). A massive, caramelized pancake shredded into pieces, dusted with powdered sugar, and served with plum roaster. Watch the pastry chefs make it in the glass-walled kitchen.

🌭 8:30 PM – Night Cap at Bitzinger Würstelstand

📍 Albertinaplatz 1, 1010 Wien | [Open in Google Maps]

The Food Order a Käsekrainer (pork sausage injected with chunks of Emmental cheese). Get it sliced with an Ottakringer beer, eating under the glowing lights of the Opera House.

🛫 Day 5: May 22 (Thursday) – Departure Morning

🥐 8:00 AM – The "Bakery Run" like a Local

📍 Any major U-Bahn station (e.g., Westbahnhof or Hauptbahnhof)

The Spot Since you leave at 10:00 AM, skip the sit-down breakfast. Stop at a Ströck or Anker bakery inside the U-Bahn station. Grab a Topfengolatsche (sweet curd cheese pastry) and a coffee to go before heading to the airport/train!
